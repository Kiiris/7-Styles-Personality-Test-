“If you're lonely when you're alone, you're in bad company.” ― Jean-Paul Sartre

The key to success for solitary-intrapersonal learners is plenty of space and silence. These students love to work on their own and concentrate best when in a quiet, peaceful environment. It’s important to check in with these learners on a regular basis however, since they may find it difficult to ask for help. If you find that your student is struggling, approach them gently and ask if there is anything they need assistance with. Below are some study tips for intrapersonal learners.

In Software Development...

Also known as intrapersonal learner, this type of learner needs quiet time alone in order to best process information and grasp concepts. Rather than thrive in a group, this type of learner may find themselves overwhelmed or distracted in a group setting. They will best benefit of reading on their own and taking the time to pause when needed to ensure comprehension. If you are a solitary learner there are an abundance of resources to research online and coding challenges you can attempt in your downtime.
