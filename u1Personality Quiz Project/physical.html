"Intelligence and skill can only function at the peak of their capacity when the body is healthy and strong. ..." - John F Kennedy

People with bodily kinesthetic learning styles learn best when they are permitted to use their tactile senses and fine and gross motor movement as part of the learning process. They often prefer direct involvement with the material they are learning rather than worksheets or reading from a book. Bodily kinesthetic learning style students understand and remember material longer when they use it in an active way.

In Software Development...

These types of learners like to move around and perform hands-on activities to truly understand new subjects, understanding things better when they are able to take something apart and put it back together, both literally and figuratively. Performing lab experiments or assignments and actively practicing the topic at hand will benefit this type of learner greatly. When it comes to coding, these learners thrive when playing with code itself. Projects that involve creating applications or building websites or fixing broken code are real-world examples that give kinesthetic types a fuller understanding of coding itself, and importantly, how to apply it.
